⚙️ Camera Settings (Photos)

  • Mode: Aperture Priority (A)
  • Aperture: f/9 (range f/9–f/13)
  • Exposure: +0.3 EV
  • ISO: 640–1000 (max 1500 in dark)
  • WB: Auto
  • Bracketing: Day → 3 brackets, ±2EV • Twilight → 5 brackets, ±1EV
  • Polarizer for glare/reflections
  • Clean lens before every shoot

📏 Tripod Height

  • Interiors: just above doorknob height.
  • Bathrooms: balance vanity + light fixture.
  • Kitchens: raise slightly (avoid cabinet undersides).
  • Exteriors: handheld high for curb + sky balance.

📸 Photo Count & Timing

Times include setup, walkthrough, and file drop/culling.

  • Lease Photos: 15 photos • ~40 min
  • < 1,000 sq ft: 10–15 photos • ~45 min
  • 1k–2k sq ft: 25–30 photos • ~1 hr
  • 2k–3k sq ft: 35–40 photos • ~1.5 hr
  • 3k–4k sq ft: 40–50 photos • ~1 hr 45 min
  • 4k–5k sq ft: 50–60 photos • ~2 hr
  • Drone Photos: 5–7 photos • ~15 min
  • Community Amenities: 10–15 photos • ~20 min

🛋️ Room Guidelines

  • All rooms: entry shot + opposite corner.
  • Front exteriors: 4–5 (straight‑on, both sides, compressed, w/ or w/o gate).
  • Bedrooms: doorway + opposite corner.
  • Primary bed: 2–3 (show flow to bath).
  • Bathrooms: usually 1 (optional reverse), 2+ for primary.
  • Closets: walk‑in only.
  • Kitchen/Dining/Living: min 3 each; include straight‑on island/table.
  • Backyard: 3–4 (include reverse looking at house).
  • Garage: only finished (check with agent).

🌅 Twilight

  • Arrive 15 min before sunset.
  • All lights/fireplace ON.
  • Prime time: 10–15 min after sunset.
  • Bracket setting: 5 brackets, ±1EV.
  • Drone if appropriate (views, hills, big lot).
  • Twilight window is only 10–15 min.
  • Order: Drone Photo/Video → Photos → Video.

🚁 Drone Basics

  • Check airspace in Aloft app and submit LAANC.
  • Wait for GPS lock → “Home Point Updated.”
  • Do NOT fly without GPS / if in Restricted Zone.
  • Watch wind → test hover before flying.
  • Compose: sky ≈ 1/3 of frame; house in lower middle third.
  • Capture variety (close + wide, house + area).
  • Highlight surroundings (ocean, city, schools, landmarks).

🎥 Video Settings

  • Resolution: 4K 60p
  • Shutter Speed: 1/125
  • ISO: Auto only if under 2500. For interiors set 12800 (2nd base ISO). Never use 2500–12800 or above 12800.
  • Aperture (F-Stop): adjust for exposure +1.3 to +2.0 (ideal +1.7). Do not let +2.0 blink.
  • Picture Profile: S-Log3
  • Grid: Rule of thirds + diagonals
  • Zebra: Lower Limit 94+
  • Peaking: On, Red
  • White Balance: Auto
  • File Format: XAVC HS 4K
  • Record Setting: 100 Mbps 4:2:2 10-bit
  • Stabilization: SteadyShot → Standard

📷 Lens Settings

  • Wides (Laowa manual focus): focus between ∞ and 3; aperture f/2.8–f/22.
  • Details (Sony 50mm f/1.4): aperture f/1.4–f/4; manually set focus for each shot.

🎬 Video Composition

  • Core wide-angle shots: Curved push-in (go-to), Straight push-in (safety), Parallax, Birdie, FPV/Dutch Angles (creative).
  • Get shots from multiple directions in each room, like with photos.
  • Always shoot from as far back as possible to maximize space.
  • Each shot must have Meat → Picture Shot → Meat: entry ramp, slowed picture shot, exit ramp.
  • Use hallways/doorways to add depth and lead into rooms.

🎯 Video Shot Framework — F.A.I.M

These parameters must be checked for every shot, and always set based on your Picture Shot (the slowed portion), not the entry/exit ramp.

  • F — Focus: use peaking (red lines) to confirm subject is sharp.
  • A — Aperture: default to lowest (usually f/2.8), adjust as needed for exposure.
  • I — ISO: Auto if it remains under 2500, otherwise 12800.
  • M — Metering: exposure meter +1.3 to +2.0 (ideal +1.7), never blinking. Adjust using ISO/aperture.

🗣️ Agent On-Camera & Scripting

Use our Script Writer GPT Bot to create scripts within our guidelines.

  • Structure: 1 Hook → 2–4 Middle Sentences → 1 Call to Action
  • Hooks: Avoid “Welcome to…”, stating the address, or mentioning bed/bath counts.
  • Call to Action: e.g. “DM me for more information” or “Contact me for a private showing.”
  • Filming Go-to Shot: Wide-angle lens, agent walking toward camera while operator backs up at same speed.
  • Alternative Shot: Start farther away and both walk toward center (but prefer walking backward with agent).
